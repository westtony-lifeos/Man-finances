The Retirement Math

What does it actually take to retire?

Right now, you have paychecks from New Life Medical and distributions from Vivex. But if one day those stop, that's when true retirement begins. From that point on, it would be just Social Security and your savings — nothing else. That's what we want to be prepared for.

There's a simple rule: you can safely withdraw 4% of your savings per year without running out of money. So the less you spend, the less you need saved.

Your Action Plan

Phase 1: Reduce Spending

Here are 7 steps to reduce your spending toward the $12,000/month target. Total potential savings: $36,500–$37,500/year.

1
This Is the Big One: Close PayPal
One action. $21,200 in annual savings. This single step can launch you toward your $2 million goal.

In 2025, $29,272 flowed through PayPal — that's $2,439/month across hundreds of transactions. Most of it was impulse purchases you probably don't remember. About $8,000 was legitimate spending (airlines, retailers) that you'll continue with credit cards.

To close: PayPal.com → Settings → Account → Close Account

This automatically cancels subscriptions (Am Apps, Appcharge, Dressly, and more) plus dozens of small purchases that add up fast.

Don't worry: Sites like American Airlines, Wayfair, and Weight Watchers all accept credit cards directly — you won't lose access to anything you actually need. And Tom can still enjoy Temu with a credit card (see Step 3).

6
Protect Your Cash — Switch to Credit Cards
A simple safety step. Debit cards pull directly from your bank accounts. Credit cards add a layer of protection between your purchases and your savings.

Benefits of using 1-2 credit cards:
• Better fraud protection — disputed charges don't touch your savings
• Cash back rewards — 2% on spending adds up to about $400/year
• Built-in limit — an extra safeguard for your savings
• Easier tracking — all spending shows up in one place
$400+/yr

Set a Cash Budget

Cash is hard to track — once it's withdrawn, we lose visibility into where it goes.

Suggested target: $200/month each ($400/month total)
Using cards for most purchases helps us see spending patterns during quarterly reviews. Keep cash for the things that need it (tips, farmers markets, small vendors).

This is more about visibility than savings — it helps us spot opportunities in the future.
