The Retirement Math — The 4% Rule

What does it actually take to retire?

There's a proven approach called the 4% rule: if you only withdraw 4% of your savings each year, your money can last forever. Here's why — your savings earn money while they sit there (around 6-7% per year in a balanced portfolio). If you take out less than they earn, you never run out. The leftover growth covers inflation and keeps your nest egg whole. So the less you spend each month, the less you need saved — and the sooner you reach "forever money."
